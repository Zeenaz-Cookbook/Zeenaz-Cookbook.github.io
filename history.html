About Dolly and Sadru Pisani


  • 1941

    Dolly, born in Dodoma, Tanzania first learned the basics of cooking from her father at a very young age and continued to pick up new skills simply by watching others. She was blessed with a natural sense of creativity and trained as a seamstress and designer. While going on to teach those crafts in Dar-es-Salaam, her passion for experimenting with different flavors in the kitchen was never far behind.

  • 1962

    She married Sadru Pisani and thirteen years later they immigrated to Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada.

  • 1985

    They opened Zeenaz Restaurant, fulfilling their dream of bringing Vancouver its first taste of East African-style Indian cuisine. It was truly a family affair: Sadru’s entrepreneurial spirit, Dolly’s incredible cooking talent and even the restaurant’s name which fuses those of their children and grandchildren. Dolly’s inventiveness with traditional dishes and unique flavours along with Sadru’s famous hospitality resulted in a loyal following, not to mention rave reviews from local food critics, Tourism Vancouver and the Lonely Planet.

  • 2003

    Dolly and Sadru opened Zeenaz Kitchen to meet the growing demand for their snack foods, pickles, and catering services. The secret recipe for their signature snack product Chevdo, a blend of puffed rice, nuts and savoury and sweet spices, has been refined to perfection over the years. It really must be tasted!

  • 2010

    The adventure began when Dolly began to transfer the wealth of knowledge onto paper resulting in her cookbook: Zeenaz – A Culinary Legacy

  • Coming Soon

    Dolly wishes to share her time and knowledge by providing cooking lessons in Vancouver
